About Encryption...

Encryption is the process of translating information into an alternate format, with the purpose of making the information unattainable to those unauthorised to access the information. Some of the earliest uses of encryption dates back to around 600 BC when the Ancient Spartans attempted to gain an advantage during warfare by sending war tactics using a device capable of communicating written messages. Both the sender and receiver of the device had joint knowledge of how to operate the device which allowed the messages to be translated only by the intended receiver. Today we rely on encryption for many things from the privacy of our online shopping details to bank records and phone passwords.
